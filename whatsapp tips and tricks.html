30 Secret WhatsApp Tips & Tricks you are probably not using

Image by rawpixel.com on Freepik

WhatsApp is one of the most popular messaging apps in the world, with over two billion active users. While many people use it on a daily basis, there are a lot of features and tricks that can make it even more useful. From pinning important chats to enabling fingerprint lock, these tips and tricks can help you get the most out of WhatsApp. In this article, we'll explore 30 tips and tricks for WhatsApp that you may not be using yet, and explain how you can use them to enhance your messaging experience.



  1. Run WhatsApp on two phones

    2. To run WhatsApp on two phones simultaneously, you can use the WhatsApp Web feature which allows you to mirror your WhatsApp chats on a second device. Here are the steps to follow:

    1. Open WhatsApp on your primary device and go to Settings > WhatsApp Web/Desktop.
    2. Tap on the "+" sign or "Link a Device" option to generate a QR code.
    3. On your second device, open a web browser and go to web.whatsapp.com.
    4. Scan the QR code on your primary device using the WhatsApp Web feature on your second device.
    5. Once the QR code is scanned, your WhatsApp account will be mirrored on your second device and you can now use WhatsApp on both devices simultaneously.

    It's important to note that your primary device must have an active internet connection for the WhatsApp Web feature to work on your second device. Additionally, using the same WhatsApp account on multiple devices can cause synchronization issues and lead to data loss, so it's best to use this feature only when necessary.

  2. Find who you talk to most on WhatsApp

    3. You can find out who you talk to most on WhatsApp by following these steps:

    1. Open WhatsApp on your mobile device.
    2. Go to theChats tab .
    3. Tap on the three dots in the top right corner to access the menu.
    4. Select "Settings".
    5. Choose "Storage and data".
    6. Tap on "Manage Storage".
    7. You'll see a list of your WhatsApp contacts and groups, sorted by the amount of storage they use for media and messages.
    8. The person or group at the top of the list is the one you talk to most on WhatsApp.

    Note that this method works on both iOS and Android devices. The steps are the same for both operating systems. However, this method only shows the amount of storage used by each contact or group, which can be a good indication of who you talk to most, but it's not an exact measure of how often you chat with them. Additionally, this method only works if you have exchanged media or messages with the contact or group, so if you haven't chatted with them recently, they may not appear at the top of the list.

  3. Use different Text Formats on WhatsApp

    4. WhatsApp allows you to use various text formats to make your messages more expressive. Here are the different text formats and how to use them:

    • Bold: To make your text bold, add an asterisk (*) to either side of the text. For example:*Hello*
    • Italic: To make your text italic, add an underscore (_) to either side of the text. For example:_Hello_
    • Underline: To underline your text, add a tilde (~) to either side of the text. For example:~Hello~
    • Monospace: To use monospace font, add three backticks (```) to either side of the text. For example: ```Hello```
    • Strikethrough: To strikethrough your text, add a tilde (~) to either side of the text. For example:~~Hello~~

    Combine different formats to make your messages even more expressive. For example, *~Hello~*would create bold and underlined text.

  4. Quickly Delete All Media from a WhatsApp Chat

    5. If you need to quickly delete all the messages, photos, videos, GIFs, contacts, locations, voice messages, documents, or stickers from a certain chat on WhatsApp, it's possible to do so. This can be useful, for example, if you want to clear out a chat without losing any of the media files. Both iOS and Android devices allow you to perform this task easily.

    To get started, go to Settings, then Storage and Data, and then Manage Storage. From there, select the specific contact or group chat you want to clear out, and then select the types of files you want to delete, such as videos or images. Finally, tap on the bin to delete them.

    Additionally, you can sort the files by size, date, or other criteria by tapping the circle in the bottom left corner after selecting the contact. This can help you quickly find the files you want to delete.

    Keep in mind that this process will delete all the selected files permanently, so make sure you want to remove them before proceeding.

  5. Save Your Data Allowance on WhatsApp

    6. If you have a limited data allowance on your mobile plan, you may want to prevent WhatsApp from using too much of it. Fortunately, you can adjust your settings to customize when media files are allowed to download, and to ensure that calls use as little data as possible.

    On both iOS and Android devices, go to Settings, then Storage and Data, and then Media Auto-Download. From there, you can choose to switch to Wi-Fi only for each type of media, such as photos, videos, and voice messages, in order to save your data.

    By doing so, WhatsApp will only download media files when you're connected to Wi-Fi, rather than using your mobile data. This can help you avoid using up your data allowance too quickly.

  6. How to Pin Important Chats on WhatsApp

    7. If you have important chats on WhatsApp that you want to keep easily accessible, you can use the "Pin Chat " feature to pin them to the top of your chat list. This can be especially useful if you have a lot of chats and don't want to scroll through them to find the most important ones.

    To pin a chat, simply swipe right on the chat and select the pin icon. The chat will then appear at the top of your chat list. You can pin multiple chats if you need to.

    If you want to unpin a chat, simply swipe right on the pinned chat and select the unpin icon. The chat will then return to its original position in your chat list.

    Using the pin feature is a quick and easy way to keep important chats easily accessible on WhatsApp.

  7. Use the Forward Messages, Photos, and Videos on WhatsApp

    8. If you want to share messages, photos, or videos with other chats on WhatsApp, you can use the "Forward" feature. This can be especially useful if you want to share something that you've received with someone else.

    To forward a message, simply hold down on the message you want to forward and select "Forward". You can then choose the chat you want to send the message to. You can forward messages to multiple chats if you need to.

    You can also forward photos and videos in a similar way. Just select the photo or video you want to forward and then select the "Forward" option. You can then choose the chat you want to send the photo or video to.

    Using the forward feature is a quick and easy way to share messages, photos, and videos with other chats on WhatsApp.

  8. Create and Use "Broadcast Lists" on WhatsApp

    9. The "Broadcast Lists" feature on WhatsApp allows you to send a message to multiple contacts at once, without having to create a group chat. This feature can be particularly useful if you want to send the same message to multiple people without having to type it out several times.

    To create a broadcast list:

    1. Open WhatsApp and go to "Chats".
    2. Select "New Broadcast".
    3. Select the contacts you want to include in the broadcast list.
    4. Tap "Create".
    5. Send a message to the broadcast list.

    Note that when you send a message to a broadcast list, it will appear as an individual message to each contact in the list, rather than as a group message. This means that each contact will receive the message as if it was sent only to them.

    The broadcast lists feature can be a useful way to send the same message to multiple contacts without having to create a group chat.

  9. The "Reply Privately" feature on WhatsApp

    10. The "Reply Privately" feature on WhatsApp allows you to reply to a specific message in a group chat without starting a new chat. This can be useful if you want to respond to a message privately or if you want to have a one-on-one conversation with someone in the group.

    1. To use the "Reply Privately" feature, swipe right on the message you want to reply to in the group chat.
    2. Then, select "Reply Privately" from the options that appear.
    3. This will open a private chat with the person who sent the message, with the original message quoted at the top of the chat.
    4. You can then type your reply and send it as you would in any other chat.

    Note that when you use the "Reply Privately" feature, the person you're responding to will be able to see the original message you're replying to, but other members of the group won't be able to see it.

    The "Reply Privately" feature can be a useful way to have private conversations within a group chat without having to start a new chat.

  10. Change the Wallpaper on WhatsApp

    11. WhatsApp has a standard wallpaper that appears as a background on all of your chats. However, you can change this wallpaper to personalize your chat experience. WhatsApp provides several options to choose from, including solid colors, your own photos, as well as a collection of images from WhatsApp itself.

    If you want to set a custom wallpaper on WhatsApp, here's what you need to do:

    iOS:
    1. Open WhatsApp and go to "Settings" > "Chats" > "Chat Wallpaper".
    2. Select "Choose a New Wallpaper".
    3. Choose from "Bright", "Dark", "Solid Colors", or "Photos".
    4. Select the image you want to use and adjust it as needed by zooming in or out.
    5. Tap "Set" to apply the wallpaper to your chats.
    Android:
    1. Open WhatsApp and go to "Settings" > "Chats" > "Chat Wallpaper".
    2. Select "Choose Wallpaper Library", "Solid Colors", "Gallery", "Default", or "No Wallpaper".
    3. If you choose "Gallery", select the image you want to use and adjust it as needed by zooming in or out.
    4. Tap "Set" to apply the wallpaper to your chats.

    Changing the wallpaper on WhatsApp is a quick and easy way to add a personal touch to your chats.

  11. Set Specific Wallpaper for a WhatsApp Chat

    12. If you want to give a specific chat a unique look, you can change the wallpaper for that chat in WhatsApp. Here's how to do it:

    iOS:

    • Open the specific chat you want to customize the wallpaper for.
    • Tap on the name of the chat at the top of the screen.
    • Select "Wallpaper & Sound" from the options.
    • Choose "Choose a New Wallpaper".
    • Select from the available options, such as "Bright", "Dark", "Solid Colors", or "Photos".

    Android:

    • Open the specific chat you want to customize the wallpaper for.
    • Tap on the three dots at the top right corner of the screen.
    • Select "Wallpaper".
    • Choose "Change" from the options.
    • Select from the available options, such as "Default", "No Wallpaper", "Solid Colors", or "Gallery".

    That's it! Now you can give your chats a more personalized look with custom wallpapers.

  12. Use WhatsApp's Disappearing Messages Feature

    13. WhatsApp's Disappearing Messages feature allows you to automatically delete any new messages in a chat after a certain period of time. However, it's important to note that the messages can still be saved elsewhere, and some messages in a chat with Disappearing Messages turned on can be manually saved. You have the option to turn on Disappearing Messages for specific chats or all chats.

    To enable Disappearing Messages for a specific chat, follow these steps:

    • On iOS and Android , open the chat and tap on the name of the person at the top.
    • Tap on "Disappearing Messages " and choose a message timer.

    To turn on Disappearing Messages for all chats, follow these steps:

    • On iOS and Android , go to Settings > Account > Privacy > Default Message Timer .
    • Choose a time interval for the disappearing messages.

  13. Use the "Search" Feature on WhatsApp

    14. The " Search " feature on WhatsApp allows you to quickly find specific messages, chats, or contacts. This can be particularly useful if you have a lot of chats or messages and need to find something specific.

    To use the " Search " feature, follow these steps:

    • Open WhatsApp and go to "Chats."
    • Tap the search icon at the top of the screen.
    • Type in what you're looking for (such as a specific message, chat, or contact) and hit "Enter" or "Search."
    • WhatsApp will search your messages, chats, and contacts and display any matches.

    Using the "Search" feature can save you a lot of time and make it easier to find what you're looking for on WhatsApp.

  14. Use the "Status" Feature on WhatsApp

    15. The "Status" feature on WhatsApp allows you to share photos, videos, or text updates with your contacts. To create a status, follow these steps:

    1. Open WhatsApp and tap on the "Status" tab at the bottom of the screen.
    2. Tap the camera icon in the bottom center of the screen to take a photo or video, or tap the pencil icon in the top right corner to type a text update.
    3. You can add text, emojis, stickers, or draw on the photo or video.
    4. Tap the green arrow icon to post your status, which will be visible to your contacts for 24 hours.

    Note that your contacts can reply to your status, and you can also view who has viewed your status by tapping on it.

    The "Status" feature can be a fun way to share moments with your contacts without having to send them individually.

  15. Access and Customize Privacy Settings on WhatsApp

    16. WhatsApp's "Privacy" settings give you control over who can see your profile picture, status, and last seen. To access the privacy settings, follow these steps:

    1. Open WhatsApp and go to "Settings".
    2. Tap on "Account".
    3. Select "Privacy".

    Once you're in the privacy settings, you can customize the following options:

    • Last Seen: Choose who can see when you were last active on WhatsApp, including everyone, contacts, or nobody.
    • Profile Photo: Choose who can see your profile photo, including everyone, contacts, or nobody.
    • Status: Choose who can see your status updates, including everyone, contacts, or nobody. You can also customize who can see your status updates by creating a custom list of contacts.
    • Blocked Contacts: View and manage a list of contacts you have blocked on WhatsApp.

    By customizing your privacy settings, you can have more control over who sees your information on WhatsApp.

  16. Enable Two-Step Verification on WhatsApp

    17. The "Two-Step Verification" feature on WhatsApp adds an extra layer of security to your account. Here's how to enable it:

    • Open WhatsApp and go to "Settings ".
    • Tap on "Account".
    • Select "Two-Step Verification".
    • Tap "Enable" and enter a 6-digit PIN .
    • Confirm the PIN and add an email address that can be used to reset your PIN if you forget it.

    Once you have enabled two-step verification, you will be prompted to enter the PIN when you try to verify your phone number on WhatsApp. This helps to prevent unauthorized access to your account.

  17. Use the "Block" feature on WhatsApp

    18. If you want to prevent someone from contacting you on WhatsApp, you can use the "Block" feature. Here's how to do it:

    1. Open WhatsApp and go to the chat with the person you want to block.
    2. Tap the three dots at the top right of the chat.
    3. Select "Block" from the dropdown menu.
    4. Confirm your choice by tapping "Block"again.

    You can also block someone from their profile page:

    1. Go to the person's profile page.
    2. Tap the three dots at the top right.
    3. Select "Block" from the dropdown menu.
    4. Confirm your choice by tapping "Block" again.

    Once you block someone, they won't be able to send you messages or see your profile updates. You can unblock them at any time by following the same steps and selecting "Unblock" instead of "Block".

  18. How to "Report" Someone feature on WhatsApp works

    19. If you encounter abusive or inappropriate behavior on WhatsApp, you can use the "Report" feature to notify the platform. Here's how to do it:

    1. Open WhatsApp and go to the chat with the person you want to report.
    2. Tap the three dots at the top right of the chat.
    3. Select " Report" from the dropdown menu.
    4. Select the reason for your report, such as "Spam" or "Abusive Content".
    5. Provide additional information if necessary.
    6. Tap "Report" to submit your report.

    WhatsApp takes reports seriously and will investigate any reported behavior that violates their community standards. Note that WhatsApp will not notify the person you report that they have been reported.

  19. Use the Pin and Unpin a Chat on WhatsApp

    20. The "Pin" feature in WhatsApp allows you to keep important chats at the top of your chat list for easy access. Here's how to pin and unpin a chat:

    1. Open WhatsApp and go to the chat you want to pin.
    2. Swipe right on the chat.
    3. Select "Pin" from the options that appear.
    4. The chat will now appear at the top of your chat list with a pin icon next to it.
    5. To unpin a chat, swipe right on the chat again.
    6. Select "Unpin" from the options that appear.
    7. The chat will now be unpinned and return to its original position in the chat list.

    Voila! You can use the pin feature to easily access your important chats without having to scroll through your entire chat list. Note that you can only pin up to three chats at a time.

  20. Use the "Archive" and "Unarchive" Chats feature

    21. The "Archive" feature allows you to hide chats from your chat list. To archive a chat, follow these steps:

    1. Swipe left on the chat that you want to archive.
    2. Tap the "Archive" button that appears on the right.
    3. Once you archive a chat, it will be moved to the "Archived Chats" folder. On Android devices, to access archived chats, scroll to the bottom of your chat list and select "Archived Chats". On iOS devices , archived chats are located at the top of the chat list.

    You can archive individual or group chats on WhatsApp. You can also archive multiple chats at once by following these steps:

    1. Long press on a chat that you want to archive.
    2. Select the other chats you want to archive.
    3. Tap on the archive icon that appears on the top bar.

    To unarchive a chat on WhatsApp, follow these steps:

    1. On Android devices Scroll to the bottom of your chat list and tap "Archived Chats" on IOS devices there is no need to scroll to the button since the Archived Chars are located on the top of the chat list.
    2. Swipe lefton the chat that you want to unarchive.
    3. Tap the "Unarchive" button that appears on the right.
    4. Once you unarchive a chat, it will be moved back to your main chat list.

    Note that you can archive as many chats as you want, but only up to 100 chats will be visible in the "Archived Chats" folder at a time. Once you exceed 100 archived chats, the oldest chats will be automatically removed from the folder.

  21. Use the Mute a Chat on WhatsApp

    22. The "Mute" feature allows you to silence notifications from a specific chat. Here's how to do it:

    1. Open WhatsApp and go to the chat that you want to mute.
    2. Swipe right on the chat.
    3. Select "Mute" from the dropdown menu.
    4. Select the duration of the mute (8 hours, 1 week, or 1 year).

    Once you mute a chat, you will no longer receive notifications for new messages in that chat. However, the chat will still appear in your chat list and you can read new messages when you open the chat.

  22. Send a Disappearing Image or Video on WhatsApp

    23. You can send an image or video that disappears once the recipient has viewed it once. This is great for photos of sensitive information like passwords or bank details. However, remember that the recipient can still take a screenshot.

    iOS

    1. Open the specific chat where you want to send the disappearing image or video.
    2. Tap on the "+" next to the message input field.
    3. Choose "Camera" to take a photo or "Photo & Video Library" to select a photo.
    4. Select the photo you want to send.
    5. Tap on the "1" next to the blue send arrow.
    6. Press the blue arrow to send.

    Android

    1. Open the specific chat where you want to send the disappearing image or video.
    2. Tap on the paperclip next to the message input field.
    3. Choose "Camera" to take a photo or "Gallery " to select a photo.
    4. Select the photo you want to send.
    5. Tap on the "1" next to the blue send arrow.
    6. Press the blue arrow to send.

    That's it! Your recipient will only be able to view the image or video once before it disappears.

  23. Use the "Export Chat" feature on WhatsApp

    24. The "Export Chat" feature on WhatsApp is a useful tool that allows you to save a copy of your chat with an individual or group as a text file. This can be helpful if you want to keep a record of important conversations or if you need to share a conversation with someone else.

    1. Go to the chat with the person or group you want to export.
    2. Tap the three dots at the top right of the chat screen.
    3. Select "More".
    4. Select "Export Chat".
    5. Choose whether you want to include media or not.
    6. Tap "Export" and the chat will be exported as a text file and saved to your device.

    Once you make your selection, the chat will be exported as a text file and saved to your device. The exported chat will include the date and time each message was sent, as well as the name of the person who sent it.

    It's important to note that exporting a chat does not delete it from your device or from the recipient's device. Also, if you have end-to-end encryption enabled, the exported chat will be encrypted as well, meaning that only the intended recipient will be able to view it.

  24. How to Manage "Group Settings" on WhatsApp

    6. Adjust group settings by tapping "Group Settings". From here, you can change whether members can edit the group info, whether media is visible to all members, and more.

    That's it! You can use the group settings feature to manage your group chats and keep them running smoothly.

  25. How to Create a Group Chat on WhatsApp

    2. Select "New Group".
    3. Select the contacts you want to include in the group.
    4. Tap "Create".
    5. Give the group a name and profile picture.
    6. Start chatting with the group members.

    You can create groups for different purposes, such as for family, friends, or work colleagues, and easily chat with multiple people at once.

    iOS:

    2. Select the specific chat.
    3. Select the specific message you want to check.
    4. Swipe right to left.
    Android:
    1. Go to "Chats".
    2. Select the specific chat.
    3. Select the specific message you want to check.
    4. Press and hold the message.
    5. Tap the info "i" icon at the top.

    By doing this, you can see when your message was delivered and read by the recipient. However, keep in mind that this feature works only if the recipient has not turned off their read receipts.

    • iOS:
      • Go to "Chats".

  28. How to "Back Up" Your Chats on WhatsApp

    29. To make sure you don't lose any of your chats if you lose your device or you switch devices, make sure you back up your chats to iCloud (for iOS users) or Google Drive (for Android users). Here's how to do it:

    1. Open WhatsApp and go to "Settings ".
    2. Select "Chats ".
    3. Select "Chat Backup ".
    4. Select "Back Up Now ".
    5. You can also select "Auto Backup " to set up automatic backups at a specific time or frequency.

    Be aware that archived chats aren't encrypted, so if you want to keep your chats private, don't archive them. Instead, you can use the "Delete " option to remove chats you no longer need.

  29. How to Create a Group Invite Link on WhatsApp

    30. The "Group Invite Link " feature allows you to invite people to a group chat using a link. Here's how to do it:

    1. Open WhatsApp and go to the group chat.
    2. Tap the group name at the top of the screen.
    3. Select "Invite to Group via Link ".
    4. Choose whether you want to generate a link that never expires or a link that expires after a certain amount of time.

    That's it! You can then share the link with anyone you want to invite to the group. When they click on the link, they will be able to join the group chat.

  30. Move WhatsApp Chats from iOS to Android

    31. Moving WhatsApp chats from iOS to Android can be a bit tricky, but here are the general steps to follow:

    1. Make sure you have the latest version of WhatsApp installed on both devices.
    2. On your iPhone, go to WhatsApp Settings > Chats > Chat Backup and tap "Back Up Now" to create a backup of your chats to iCloud.
    3. On your Android device, download and install a third-party app called WazzapMigrator from the Google Play Store.
    4. On your iPhone, use the "Share Chat History" feature to send the chat history you want to transfer via email.
    5. Open the email on your Android device and download the chat history file to your device.
    6. Open the WazzapMigrator app on your Android device and follow the on-screen instructions to import the chat history file and complete the transfer.

    It's important to note that this method may not transfer all chat data, such as media files or group chats, and there is a small chance of data loss or corruption during the transfer process. Therefore, it's recommended to do a test run with a small number of chats before transferring all your WhatsApp data.
